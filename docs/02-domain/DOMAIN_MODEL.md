# Career OS — Domain Model v1.0

## Purpose

The Domain Model defines the language, objects, relationships, and rules that describe Career OS.

This document is independent of programming languages, databases, APIs, and UI. It models the real world that Career OS understands.

---

# 1. Core Design Principles

## Principle 1 — Object First

The platform models real-world objects rather than screens or database tables.

Examples:

* Person
* Organization
* Project
* Opportunity
* Goal

---

## Principle 2 — Relationships Matter

Relationships are as important as objects.

Career value emerges from how things connect.

---

## Principle 3 — AI Reasons Over the Graph

AI never reasons over isolated records.

It reasons over connected objects.

---

## Principle 4 — Explainability

Every recommendation must reference:

* Evidence
* Knowledge
* Goals
* Relationships
* Confidence
* Tradeoffs

---

## Principle 5 — Time Matters

Every important object has history.

Nothing important is static.

---

# 3. First-Class Objects

## Person

Represents an individual relevant to the user's professional journey.

Examples

* Mentor
* Professor
* Recruiter
* Researcher
* Investor
* Hiring Manager
* Author
* Collaborator

Core properties

* Identity
* Biography
* Expertise
* Organizations
* Research Areas
* Interests
* Contact Information (if available)
* Timeline

---

## Organization

Represents institutions.

Examples

* Company
* University
* Laboratory
* VC Firm
* Research Institute
* Government Agency

---

## Opportunity

Represents any external opportunity capable of advancing long-term goals.

Examples

* Internship
* Full-time Job
* PhD Position
* Fellowship
* Scholarship
* Research Assistantship
* Conference
* Competition

Lifecycle

Discovered

↓

Researching

↓

Preparing

↓

Applied

↓

Assessment

↓

Interview

↓

Offer

↓

Accepted

↓

Completed

↓

Alumni

---

## Project

Represents a structured investment that creates career value.

Projects build assets.

Projects produce deliverables.

Projects support goals.

---

## Goal

Represents desired long-term outcomes.

Examples

* Secure Full-Time Position
* Fully Funded PhD
* Become an Exceptional Programmer
* Build Research Portfolio

Goals contain:

* Milestones
* Success metrics
* Supporting projects
* Supporting opportunities
* Supporting skills

---

## Decision

Represents meaningful career decisions.

Contains

* Context
* Alternatives
* Evidence
* Risks
* Recommendation
* Final Choice
* Outcome
* Reflection

---

## Knowledge

Represents reusable understanding developed by the user.

Examples

* Research summaries
* Meeting notes
* Technical insights
* Career lessons
* Market observations

Knowledge is connected—not duplicated.

---

## Document

Represents structured artifacts.

Examples

* Resume
* Cover Letter
* Transcript
* Research Paper
* Presentation
* Proposal

---

## Skill

Represents measurable capabilities.

Examples

* Python
* MATLAB
* Consulting
* Financial Modeling
* Semiconductor Physics
* Machine Learning

---

## Asset

Represents long-term career capital.

Examples

* Research Profile
* Programming Ability
* Portfolio
* Network
* Leadership
* Communication
* Interview Readiness

Assets are strengthened through projects, opportunities, and deliberate practice.
