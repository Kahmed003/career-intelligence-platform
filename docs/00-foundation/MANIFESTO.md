1. Why Career OS Exists

This should answer one question.

Why did we spend years building this instead of using Notion, LinkedIn, Gmail, Google Calendar, Trello, etc.?

Example:

Modern professionals live across dozens of disconnected systems. Opportunities, knowledge, relationships, documents, emails, and long-term goals become fragmented. Career OS exists to unify them into a single intelligent operating system that helps people make better career decisions over decades.

Notice...

That's philosophy.

Not requirements.

2. The Problem We Believe Exists

Not software problems.

Human problems.

Examples:

People forget opportunities.
Relationships decay.
Knowledge becomes buried.
Important decisions are made with incomplete information.
Daily urgency crowds out long-term growth.
3. What We Believe

These become permanent principles.

For example:

We believe...

Knowledge compounds.

Relationships compound.

Skills compound.

Good decisions compound.

Small consistent improvements outperform bursts of productivity.

Technology should augment human judgment.

AI should explain itself.

Everything should have context.

Nothing important should be forgotten.

Notice...

These are beliefs.

Not features.

Things like:

6. Engineering Philosophy

Things like:

Prefer simplicity.
Document decisions.

7. User Philosophy

This is something I don't think I've ever seen in a PRD.

Who is Career OS built for?

Not demographics.

Mindset.

For example:

Career OS is built for people who...

think long term.
are curious.
invest in themselves.
value learning.
build relationships intentionally.
care about evidence.
make thoughtful decisions.

That becomes the cultural identity of the product.

8. What Career OS Is NOT

This section is incredibly important.

For example:

Career OS is NOT

another to-do list.
another CRM.
another calendar.
another note-taking application.
another AI chatbot.

It is an operating system connecting all of them.

This helps prevent feature creep later.
